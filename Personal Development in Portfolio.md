# Personal Development in Portfolio
When working with learning outcomes and formative feedback sessions, students need to periodically self-evaluate their progression in regards to these learning outcomes. This document provides an example on such a self-evaluation, that deliberately uses an analogy rather than a real learning outcome but demonstrates how the student could describe their current level and defend that position.

### Given learning outcome: The student is able to cook and prepare a traditional spaghetti dish and then eat it.
 - **cook** refers to applying heat in a controlled environment to change the base ingredients into a meal.
 - **prepare** means that the result is positioned on a plate in a way that is appealing to the consumer.
 - **traditional** implies that the taken process of cooking and preparing is supported by historical sources.
 - **eating** refers to the action of consuming the result in way that is effective and does not produce a mess.

---

### Learning Outcome: The student is able to cook and prepare a traditional spaghetti dish and then eat it.
I already knew that spaghetti is an Italian dish, so in the presentation assignment about the history of spaghetti it was easy for me to report about my knowledge, and teacher Hans agreed that my knowledge is correct `[@Assignment: Spaghetti-History]`. During the group assignment to create spaghetti pasta from its base ingredients I realized that length is important, and suggested a small research into different pasta lengths to my group members. At the in-class discussion, pasta lengths were often mentioned and later teacher Eva pointed out that it was a great idea of me to introduce this, as it is indeed important, according to authentic Italian tradition, to ensure that the spaghetti are the correct length `[@Notes: In-class discussion]`. We then set to cook the spaghetti, which was not so hard for me because I have made tortellini before, and the process is similar. Teacher Bas provided feedback on my work and mentioned several times that the steps of my cooking process are excellent  and comply with historical sources `[@Assignment: Cooking pasta]`. It turned out that eating spaghetti is not so easy. I started with just a fork, which appeared te be near impossible and ultimately I decided to use a knife to cut the spaghetti into smaller pieces. Teacher Mandy commented that this is generally not done, and Italians would ask for a spoon `[@Assignment: Eating-Spaghetti]`. It remains rather unclear to me what the use of the spoon is, but in my challenge plan I have dedicated some time on investigating this, which teacher Tim acknowledged is a good idea `[@Challenge: Plan]`. I currently rate myself at the level of Beginning, but I suspect that the spoon investigation will help me eat the spaghetti in a way that helps me reach the level of Proficient for this learning outcome.
